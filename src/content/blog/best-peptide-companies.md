## How to Verify Any Vendor

Regardless of who you order from, here's a checklist:

1. **Ask for the COA before ordering.** Any legitimate vendor will provide batch-specific Certificates of Analysis. If they can't or won't, walk away.
2. **Check the testing method.** HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography) is the standard. Mass spectrometry is even better. "Lab tested" without specifying the method means nothing.
3. **Look for independent testing.** In-house testing is better than nothing, but third-party verification from a CLIA-certified or ISO-accredited lab is what you really want.
4. **Read Reddit and Trustpilot.** Vendor-curated testimonials are worthless. Go where people actually complain — r/Peptides, Trustpilot, and research forums.
5. **Check the packaging.** Peptides should arrive lyophilized (freeze-dried powder), properly sealed, and ideally shipped with cold packs during warm months. Wet or pre-reconstituted vials from a vendor are a red flag.
